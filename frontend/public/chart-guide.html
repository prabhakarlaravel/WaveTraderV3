NIFTY BANK · Understanding every element on your chart

1 Order Blocks (Pink Bands at Top)

Order Blocks are price zones where big institutions (banks, funds) placed large orders. When price returns to these zones, it often reverses because there are still unfilled orders waiting.

On your chart: The pink bands near ₹52,250 are SUPPLY zones — price went down from here before. If price reaches this zone again, expect sellers to step in.

3 Wave 1 — New Trend Confirmed

Wave 1 is the first strong push in the new direction. On your chart, after wave C hit ₹50,100, buyers stepped in and pushed price up to ~₹51,700.

The solid circle border around "1" means the engine has confirmed this wave — enough price action validates it as a real impulse, not just noise.

4 Fibonacci Retracement Levels

Fibonacci levels predict where price will pull back to before continuing. The engine measures the distance from Wave C (low) to Wave 1 (high) and draws horizontal lines at key ratios:

38.2% Shallow pullback — strong trend keeps going
50.0% Half retracement — very common bounce zone
61.8% (Golden) Best buy zone — used by professionals worldwide
💡 HOW TO USE

After Wave 1 is confirmed, wait for price to pull back to the 38.2%–61.8% zone. That's your entry for Wave 3 (the biggest and most profitable wave!).

5 Forming Wave (1? — The Pulsing Dot)

The dashed circle with "1?" means the engine thinks a new wave might be forming, but there's not enough data yet to confirm it. The question mark means "possibly wave 1".

The pulsing dot tracks where the current live price is — it moves in real-time as new candles come in every 30 seconds.

💡 WHAT TO WATCH FOR

When the dashed circle turns into a solid circle, the wave is confirmed. Until then, treat it as a "heads up" — not a signal to trade yet.

6 Yellow Projection Cone

The yellow dashed triangle shows the projected price range for where the new impulse wave should travel. Think of it as a "corridor" — price is expected to stay within this cone as the new waves (1-2-3-4-5) develop.

If price breaks above the cone → the move is stronger than expected (very bullish).
If price breaks below the cone → the wave count may be wrong (re-evaluate).

7 Invalidation Level (Red Line — ₹50,105)

This is your safety line. Elliott Wave theory has strict rules — if certain price levels break, the entire wave count becomes invalid.

The red "0" circle at the bottom means "if price goes here, everything resets to zero — start counting again."

⚠️ TRADING RULE

Never hold a long (buy) trade if price drops below the invalidation level. This is where you place your stop-loss.
