⚠️ Three Cardinal Rules (MUST never be broken)

Rule 1 — Wave 2

Wave 2 must NEVER retrace beyond the start of Wave 1. If price goes past the origin of Wave 1, the count is invalid.

Bullish: W2 low > W1 origin
Bearish: W2 high < W1 origin

Rule 2 — Wave 3

Wave 3 must NEVER be the shortest impulse wave. It is typically the longest and most powerful wave in the entire sequence.

W3 length > W1 length
W3 length > W5 length (usually)

Rule 3 — Wave 4

Wave 4 must NOT overlap Wave 1's price territory. The low of Wave 4 should stay above the high of Wave 1 (in a bull).

Bullish: W4 low > W1 high
Bearish: W4 high < W1 low

📝 Guidelines (common but not mandatory)

Alternation

Wave 2 and Wave 4 should alternate in form. If W2 is a sharp correction, W4 should be a flat (sideways) correction, and vice versa.

Wave 3 Extension

One impulse wave typically extends (is much longer than the others). Most commonly it's Wave 3. Extended W3 is usually 1.618x or 2.618x of W1.

Wave 5 Target

Wave 5 often equals Wave 1 in length. If W3 extended, W5 tends to be 0.618x of the W1-to-W3 distance.
